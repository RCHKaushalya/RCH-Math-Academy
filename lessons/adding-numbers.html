Adding numbers by making ten

Making ten is a helpful strategy because ten is easy to add with. Break one number apart, make ten, then add what is left.

Learning goal

Add two small numbers by making a group of ten first.

The idea

If one number is close to ten, borrow just enough from the other number to make ten. Then add the leftover amount.

7 8 9 10 11 12

Worked example

Find 7 + 5.

  1. Seven needs three more to make ten.
  2. Split five into three and two.
  3. Now 7 + 5 becomes 7 + 3 + 2.
  4. 7 + 3 = 10, and 10 + 2 = 12.

So 7 + 5 = 12.

Common mistake

After making ten, remember to add the leftover part. In 7 + 5, the leftover part is two.

Practice

Question 1

What is 8 + 4?

Answer: 12

Question 2

What is 9 + 6?

Answer: 15

Question 3

In 6 + 7, how many more does 6 need to make 10?

Answer: 4
