# Monetization and AdSense Plan

Document Status: Draft  
Prepared On: 2026-06-28  
Project: Math Tutor Web Application

## 1. Monetization Objective

The primary monetization objective is to earn advertising revenue through Google AdSense while keeping the learning experience free and respectful.

The product must prioritize learning value first. Ads should support the platform financially without making lessons harder to read or practice.

The platform includes content for young learners, including nursery-level users who may be under 13. Child-directed sections must be treated separately from general-audience and advanced mathematics sections.

## 2. AdSense Feasibility

AdSense is possible for this project, but approval is not automatic. Google expects publishers to have original content, meet program policies, and be old enough to own an account. The submitted site should also allow access to the HTML source code so AdSense can verify and review the site.

The site should not apply for AdSense until it has:

- Original lessons.
- Enough useful pages.
- Clear navigation.
- About and Contact pages.
- Privacy Policy.
- Terms of Use.
- Educational disclaimer.
- No copied or automatically generated low-value content.

## 3. AdSense Compliance Requirements

The site must follow these rules:

- Do not click your own ads.
- Do not ask users to click ads.
- Do not reward users for viewing or clicking ads.
- Do not place ads where they look like navigation, quiz controls, or lesson content.
- Do not use artificial traffic sources.
- Do not use pop-ups or deceptive ad behavior.
- Do not publish prohibited or unsafe content.
- Do not use personalized ads in child-directed sections.
- Do not place ads inside nursery activities.
- Review child privacy and age-restricted ad treatment requirements before monetizing pages intended for young children.

## 4. Recommended Ad Placement Strategy

Ad placement should begin conservatively.

Initial placements after approval:

- One responsive ad after the lesson introduction.
- One ad between lesson content and practice.
- One ad near the end of long lessons.

Ads should not be placed:

- Inside math steps where they interrupt reasoning.
- Directly beside answer buttons.
- In a way that causes layout shifts.
- Above every lesson before the learner sees useful content.
- On nursery activity screens.
- Near child-directed tap targets or game controls.

## 4.1 Child-Directed Content Rule

Nursery and early-childhood learning pages should launch without ads. If monetization is later considered for those pages, the project must review current COPPA, AdSense, and local child privacy requirements first. Google provides age-restricted treatment options for sites or ad requests, but those tools do not remove the publisher's legal obligations.

## 5. Required Policy Pages

Before applying for AdSense, create:

- `/about`
- `/contact`
- `/privacy-policy`
- `/terms`
- `/disclaimer`

Privacy Policy should mention:

- Cookies.
- Analytics, if used.
- Advertising partners, if used.
- How users can contact the site owner.

## 6. Revenue Expectations

AdSense revenue depends on:

- Number of visitors.
- Country of visitors.
- Topic value to advertisers.
- Page engagement.
- Ad viewability.
- Search traffic quality.

The first financial goal should be approval and measurement, not immediate income. The platform should publish useful lessons consistently before expecting meaningful revenue.

## 7. SEO and Content Strategy

AdSense works best when paired with organic traffic.

Recommended content strategy:

- Publish complete lessons for specific search queries.
- Target long-tail topics such as "how to add fractions with unlike denominators".
- Include worked examples and practice questions.
- Add internal links between related lessons.
- Avoid thin pages with only definitions.
- Update lessons based on search performance.
